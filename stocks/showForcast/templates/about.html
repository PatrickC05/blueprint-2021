The Impact of Food Waste

According to the article, Food Waste Facts , by Stop Wasting Food, as of 2020, 925 million people worldwide are starving. However, ⅓ of all food produced each year is wasted. This totals to be about 1.3 billion tons of food, which could feed 3 billion people. Thus, if we were to not waste roughly 31% of the food that we waste each year, then we could feed 925 million people. Additionally, food waste produces 4.4 gigatons of greenhouse gasses per year, being the 3 largest source of greenhouse gasses. Furthermore, as stated in the article, 10 Food Waste Facts , by Olio, “25% of the world’s fresh water supply is used to grow food that is never eaten”. Seeing how large of an impact food waste has and how rarely it is discussed, it is important that we look to spread food waste awareness and reduce the amount of food that is wasted around the world!

Reducing Food Waste

With food waste being such a large issue, there are multiple ways that we can reduce the amount of food that we waste. One way is by trying to solve the problem at its core, and only taking as much food as you need. Whether this be at the dinner table or while shopping for food, making sure that you only take as much food as you need will ensure that you will have a minimal amount of food waste. Additionally, saving leftovers is a very effective way to reduce the amount of food that we waste. Instead of throwing away food after a meal, saving leftovers in tupperware and storing them in the fridge is a great way to reduce your food waste and potentially the amount of cooking that you need to do! Another way by which we can strive to reduce food waste is by donating food. Food rescue organizations like 412 Food Rescue , City Harvest , and Stem4Free , all help in this effort, by making sure that leftover food is rescued as opposed to thrown away. Likewise, composting can also be an amazing way to reduce food waste. As opposed to throwing away leftover food, composting can enrich soil and prevent food from going to landfills, in turn, preventing greenhouse gas emissions. Although these aren’t the only ways to reduce the amount of food that we waste, they are definitely useful strategies that we can employ.
